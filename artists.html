The People Behind the Ink

Notable Artists

I wanted to include a page about actual tattoo artists because I think it's easy to forget that tattooing is an art form with real history and real masters. These are some of the people who shaped the industry and some newer artists who are pushing it forward.

The Legends

Norman "Sailor Jerry" Collins
Honolulu, Hawai'i · 1911–1973

This is probably the most famous tattoo artist in American history. He worked out of a shop in Chinatown in Honolulu which is wild because that's right here. He basically invented what we think of as American Traditional tattooing by combining Western sailor imagery with Japanese techniques he learned from masters in Asia. His flash sheets are still used in shops everywhere.

Don Ed Hardy
California · b. 1945

Ed Hardy studied under Sailor Jerry and also trained with Japanese tattoo masters which made him one of the most technically diverse artists of his generation. He went to the San Francisco Art Institute which helped bring a real fine art perspective to tattooing. Most people know the name from the brand but he was a serious artist long before that.

Horiyoshi III
Yokohama, Japan · b. 1946

One of the most respected living tattoo artists in Japan. He does traditional irezumi full body suits that take years to complete. His work is considered museum quality and he only takes clients by appointment. If you look up his work online it's genuinely incredible — every image tells a full story.

Lyle Tuttle
San Francisco · 1931–2019

Tuttle was really important for bringing tattoos into mainstream culture. He tattooed a lot of celebrities and musicians in the 60s and 70s at a time when tattoos were still considered very fringe. He helped change the public image of what a tattooed person looked like.

Contemporary Artists Worth Knowing

Nikko Hurtado
California

One of the best color realism artists working today. His portraits look like actual photographs — the depth and detail are kind of unbelievable. He's done a lot of pop culture work (superheroes, movie characters) but his technique works for anything.

Dr. Woo
Los Angeles

Probably the most famous fine line artist right now. His work combines super delicate linework with nature elements and geometric shapes. He's tattooed a ton of celebrities and helped make fine line tattooing trendy. His Instagram is worth looking at just as an example of what's possible with single-needle work.

Sasha Unisex
Moscow / Berlin

Known for incredibly precise geometric blackwork and dotwork. The patterns are almost architectural — it looks like math turned into art. A lot of their designs reference sacred geometry and mandala traditions but in a very modern way.

Keone Nunes
O'ahu, Hawai'i

A really important artist to include since this site is made in Hawaii. Keone Nunes is one of the leading practitioners of kakau which is traditional Hawaiian tattooing. This practice was suppressed for over a century and he has worked to bring it back as a living tradition connected to Hawaiian culture and ancestry rather than just a decorative style.

The Pacific Tradition

I wanted to add a separate note about Pacific tattooing because I think it deserves more attention than just a card in the artists section. The traditions from Hawaii, Samoa, Tonga, and Maori culture are not the same as Western tattooing — they come from completely different histories and carry spiritual and genealogical meaning.

Artists like Keone Nunes aren't just making art — they're helping people reconnect with their ancestry. The mark carries the story of who someone is and where they come from. That feels really different from just picking something off a flash sheet.
